Why understanding date labels matters

Date label confusion is a major contributor to household food waste. Many people throw away perfectly good food because they misunderstand what the dates mean.

  • Reduce waste: Understanding labels can cut household food waste by up to 20%.
  • Save money: Avoid throwing away food that's still perfectly safe to eat.
  • Stay safe: Know when food is actually unsafe versus just past peak quality.
  • Environmental impact: Less waste means fewer resources used in food production.

Types of date labels

There are two main types of date labels you'll encounter on food products, each serving a different purpose.

  • Best Before: Indicates when food is at its peak quality, not when it becomes unsafe.
  • Expiration Date: The last date the product should be consumed for safety reasons.
  • Use By: Similar to expiration date, indicates when food should be consumed for safety.
  • Sell By: Tells retailers when to remove products from shelves, not when consumers should stop eating them.

Safety vs quality

Understanding the difference between safety and quality is crucial for making smart food decisions.

Safety first

Expiration dates are about food safety - don't consume after this date.

Quality matters

Best before dates are about peak quality, not safety.

Use your senses

Look, smell, and taste food to determine if it's still good.

Storage matters

Proper storage extends the life of many foods beyond their dates.

Temperature control

Keep perishables properly refrigerated to maintain safety.

Time sensitive

Some foods are more time-sensitive than others.

Practical examples

Here are real-world examples of how to handle different types of foods and their date labels.

  • Dry goods (pasta, rice, beans): Often safe well beyond "best before" dates if stored properly.
  • Canned foods: Can last for years beyond their dates if the can is undamaged.
  • Dairy products: Use your senses - sour milk is obvious, but yogurt may be fine past its date.
  • Fresh produce: No date labels, so rely on appearance, smell, and texture.

Smart Tips for Understanding Date Labels

Follow these guidelines to make informed decisions about food safety and quality based on date labels.

  • Trust your senses: Look, smell, and taste food before deciding to throw it away.
  • Store properly: Follow storage instructions to extend food life.
  • Freeze when possible: Many foods can be frozen before their dates to extend shelf life.
  • Plan meals: Use foods approaching their dates in your meal planning.
